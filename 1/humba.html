Humba (Bisaya Version)

Enjoy a comforting taste of the southern Philippines with this Bisaya-style Humba. Tender pork belly is slowly simmered in a flavorful mix of soy sauce, vinegar, and Sprite, giving it that perfect balance of sweet and savory. The addition of banana blossoms and salted black beans adds a unique twist, while garlic, onions, and peppercorns bring out a rich aroma. Every bite of the pork is packed with flavor, making it the perfect dish to share with loved ones over a plate of warm rice. This is home-cooked goodness at its finest!

PREPARATION

Difficulty

Medium

Serves

10

Time

2 hrs 15 mins

INSTRUCTIONS

Pork belly with black beans and banana blossoms stewed in soy sauce and lemon lime soda. This is a popular Filipino dish in the southern parts of the Philippines!!

Ingredients :

Pork belly, cubed 4 lbs.

Knorr Pork Cube 1 pc

Clear soft drink (Sprite) 3 cups

Water 1 cup

Banana blossoms 2 oz

Salted black beans 2 tbsp

White vinegar 3 tbsp

Soy sauce 1/2 cup

Onion, chopped 1 pc

Brown sugar 2 tbsp

Garlic cloves, crushed and chopped 6 pcs

Crushed peppercorn 1/2 tsp

Steps in Making :

Heat a wok or pan. Sear pork belly until it turns brown and enough oil gets extracted from the fat. Remove the pork from the wok. Set aside.

Heat around 3 tablespoons of oil. Sauté garlic and onion until the onion softens.

Add the pork back into the wok and pour-in soy sauce. Sauté for 1 minute.

Add vinegar. Continue sautéing until liquid dries-up.

Pour clear soft drinks into the wok. Cover and let boil. Afterwards, add crushed peppercorn. Adjust heat to low. Continue boiling for 35 minutes.

Add banana blossoms and salted black beans. Cover the wok and continue cooking until the pork gets tender. Note: you can add water if needed.

Add brown sugar. Stir. Cook for 1 minute. Then, transfer to a serving plate. Serve with warm rice. Share and enjoy!
