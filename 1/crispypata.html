Crispy Pata

Crispy Pata is a Filipino favorite that’s perfect for any gathering. Imagine a whole pig leg cooked until the skin is irresistibly crunchy, with juicy meat that’s been simmered with bay leaves, peppercorns, and a hint of garlic. The process gives you that amazing golden-brown crunch on the outside while keeping the meat tender and flavorful inside. Whether you’re serving it with rice and atcharang papaya, or enjoying it as a delicious beer snack, Crispy Pata is always a hit. Don’t forget to dip it in a spicy vinegar mix for an extra kick!

PREPARATION

Difficulty

Medium

Serves

4

Time

1 hr 30 mins

INSTRUCTIONS

Crispy Pata or crispy pork leg is a popular Filipino pork dish. This dish can be eaten as a main dish along with rice and atcharang papaya. People also consume it as beer food or pulutan. It is best when dipped in a spicy vinegar mixture.

Ingredients :

Whole pig leg 1 pc

Dried bay leaves 6 pcs

Whole peppercorn 2 tbsp

Star anise (optional) 4 pcs

Salt 6 tsp

Ground black pepper 2 tsp

Garlic powder 2 tsp

Water 12 cups

Cooking oil 6 cups

Steps in Making :

Pour water in a cooking pot then let boil. After that, put-in dried bay leaves, whole peppercorn, star anise, and 4 teaspoons of salt.

Add the whole pig’s legs in the cooking pot then simmer until the leg becomes tender (about 45 to 60 minutes).

Remove the tender leg from the cooking pot and set aside until the temperature goes down. Then, rub the leg with garlic powder, ground black pepper, and remaining salt. Let stand for 15 minutes to absorb the rub.

Heat a clean large cooking pot (preferably with cover) and pour-in cooking oil. When the oil becomes hot, deep fry the rubbed pork leg. Continue cooking in medium heat until one side becomes crispy, and then cautiously flip the leg to crisp the other side. Note: Be extra careful in doing this procedure.

Turn-off the heat; remove the crispy pork leg; and transfer it to a wide serving plate, and serve with atcharang papaya and soy sauce – vinegar dipping sauce.

Share and enjoy!
