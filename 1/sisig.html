Pork Sisig

Pork Sisig is a crowd-favorite Filipino dish, loved for its crispy, flavorful bite. Made from a mix of pig ears, snout, and pork belly, these cuts are boiled with bay leaves and ginger, then grilled or fried until perfectly crispy. Chopped into bite-sized pieces, they're tossed with a tangy, creamy dressing made from mayonnaise, liver spread, vinegar, and a squeeze of fresh lime. With minced onions and a dash of Knorr Liquid Seasoning, this sizzling dish is packed with flavor. Whether served as a main dish or appetizer, Pork Sisig is always a hit, especially when shared with family and friends!

PREPARATION

Difficulty

Medium

Serves

5

Time

1 hr 30 mins

INSTRUCTIONS

Pork sisig is a popular Filipino dish. It can be considered as a main dish or an appetizer. The composition of this dish is mainly of pig’s parts such as minced pork meat, ears, and face!!

Ingredients :

Pig ears 1 lb.

Pig snout 1 lb.

Pork belly 1 lb.

Onion, minced 2 pcs

Bay Leaves 3 pcs

Salt 2 tsp

Ginger, crushed 4 thumbs

Water 1 qt.

Dressing :

Lady's Choice Mayonnaise 1 1/2 cup

Sukang iloko 2 tbsp

Ground black pepper 1/4 tsp

Salt 1 tsp

Sugar 1 tsp

Liver spread 1/4 cup

Limes 2 pcs

Knorr Liquid Seasoning 1 tbsp

Steps in Making :

Combine pig ears, snout, pork belly, ginger, bay leaves, 2 teaspoons salt, and 1 quart of water in a cooking pot. Boil for 1 hour. Remove from the pot and drain the water.

Heat-up your grill. Start grilling the pig parts for around 5 minutes per side. Remove from the grill afterwards and let it cool down.

Make the dressing by combining all the dressing ingredients. Mix well.

Chop the grilled pig parts into small pieces. In a large mixing bowl, combine the chopped pork with onion and the dressing mixture. Toss until all the ingredients are well blended.

Transfer to a serving plate. Serve with your favorite drink. Share and enjoy!
