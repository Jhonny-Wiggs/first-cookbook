Pork Menudo

Pork Menudo is my ultimate comfort food—so much so that I can’t go more than a month without having it! This hearty Filipino stew features tender pork and rich pig liver, cooked with diced potatoes, carrots, and sliced hotdogs in a savory tomato sauce. The blend of soy sauce, a splash of lemon, and a pinch of sugar creates a perfect balance of flavors, while garlic, onions, and bay leaves add depth and aroma. It’s a warm, satisfying dish that feels like a big, comforting hug, and I find it simply irresistible whether enjoyed on its own or served with rice.

PREPARATION

Difficulty

Medium

Serves

6

Time

1 hr 10 mins

INSTRUCTIONS

Pork Menudo is the number one on the list of my comfort foods and it’s hard for me to last a month without trying one – literally. Some would say that I am addicted to this food while others would use the term “obsession”, I just simply look at it as a necessity – a basic necessity that I cannot live without.

Ingredients :

Pork 2 lbs.

Pig liver 1/4 lbs.

Potatoes, diced 1 cup

Carrot, diced 1 pc

Soy sauce 1/2 cup

Lemon 1/2 pc

Onion, chopped 1 pc

Garlic cloves, minced 3 pcs

Sugar 1 tsp

Tomato sauce 3/4 cup

Water 1 cup

Hotdogs, sliced diagonally 4 pcs

Cooking oil 2 tbsp

Dried bay leaves 2-3 pcs

Salt and pepper to taste

Steps in Making :

Combine pork, soysauce, and lemon in a bowl. Marinate for at least 1 hour.

Heat oil in a pan and then saute garlic and onion.

Add the marinated pork. Cook for 5 to 7 minutes. Then pour in tomato sauce and water and then add the bay leaves.Let boil and simmer for 30 minutes to an hour depending on the toughness of the pork. Note: Add water as necessary.

Add-in the liver and hot dogs.Cook for 5 minutes and put-in potatoes, carrots, sugar,salt, and pepper. Stir and cook for 8 to 12 minutes.

Serve. Share and enjoy!
